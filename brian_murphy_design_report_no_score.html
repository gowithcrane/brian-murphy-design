What "AI Readiness" actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether you use ChatGPT. It's about whether your studio is set up to actually benefit from AI – so it can give you back hours each week on the around-the-work without ever touching the craft itself.

For a solo design practice, that means AI sits on the admin, research, and communication side of the business – the quoting, the project tracking, the client emails, the kickoff docs. The creative judgment your clients are paying for stays exactly where it should: with you.

For that to work, five things need to be in place:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't help if your project history lives in paper files and notebooks.
  • You know where your time is going. The repetitive tasks you can name are the tasks you can hand off to a system instead of redoing each time.
  • You know your customers. Who they are, what they want, and how to reach them. AI can't help you serve people you don't understand.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your process lives only in your head, there's nothing for a system to draft against.
  • You have momentum. You've tried a few things, you're curious, and you want to move. This is the input that makes the rest of the system actually get used.

The five conditions below are how we read where Brian Murphy Design is today. The bigger the gap on any one condition, the more leverage a small change can unlock.

Simple Project Tracker

Foundation · Replaces paper files

"What's open right now?" becomes a five-second answer. "Past logo projects under $5K?" becomes a filter. Module 3 also reads from this – without a structured project history, an AI assistant has nothing to ground on.

2

AI-Assisted Quote & Proposal Builder

Time leverage · Biggest daily time sink

Quotes get written from scratch every time. This is where the day disappears, and it's the most direct ROI in the build. We pull together a library of your past quotes – logos, brand identity, illustration, ad campaigns, theatre and nonprofit work – and set up a system where you describe a new project in 2-3 sentences and get a polished draft back in five minutes. Scope, deliverables, timelines, terms, IP and usage – all drafted in your voice. You make every final pricing call. The system just stops asking you to retype the scaffolding.

3

Personal AI Assistant Setup

Communication leverage · Solo-operator unlock

An AI workspace – Claude or ChatGPT, we'd pick together – configured for your voice, your project history, your common client categories (nonprofits, theatres, civic groups, small businesses), and your pricing logic. Used for the around-the-work that solo designers can't hand to anyone else: client emails, kickoff docs, project recaps and case-study drafts, social posts when you have a piece worth showing. Used narrowly at first, broadens only when you decide it's earned it.

4

Reputation & Referral Capture

Demand · Direct answer to the magic wand

You ask for referrals "sometimes, and informally" – that's the gap between the demand you have and the demand you could have. This module turns it into a deliberate three-touch sequence after every project closes: a thank-you, a review or testimonial ask, and a "who else might need this?" nudge. Three short messages, in your voice, triggered by the project tracker. The Beverly network you've built over decades – Rotary, Cabot, Arts Fest, Beverly Village – is denser than it looks. Most of your next-best clients are one introduction away from a current one. Modules 1-3 give you time back. Module 4 turns that time into more work landing.
